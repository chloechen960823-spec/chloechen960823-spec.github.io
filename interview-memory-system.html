01

Starting from a real pain point

I did not need prettier interview answers. I needed a memory system.

The problem was not a lack of effort. After several projects, job applications and interview prep sessions, the useful evidence was scattered across notes, screenshots and conversations. Under pressure, it became hard to retrieve the right story, choose the right angle and say it in a voice that still sounded like me.

Product framing

A small product, not a prompt dump.

I reframed the tool as a retrieval and transformation loop: capture real memories, attach them to reusable story angles, map those angles to interview questions, then create language versions that can be practiced, edited and trusted.

Vibe coding workflow

Conversational building, but with product judgment.

I used Codex as a coding partner: describe the intent, inspect the result in the browser, identify UX gaps, rewrite the interaction model, and repeat. The design work moved from drawing static screens to shaping behavior, data structure, edge cases and privacy boundaries.

Trust model

Every answer needs a provenance state.

Because the product handles personal career stories, the key design decision was to make answer reliability visible. A polished paragraph is not finished until it can be traced back to a real experience and spoken naturally.

Privacy by design

The public portfolio does not expose the private library.

The case study shows the product architecture, interaction model and AI collaboration process with anonymized examples. Real interview answers, personal stories and application material stay outside the public site. This constraint became part of the UX: useful enough to demonstrate, careful enough to be trustworthy.

What this project demonstrates

A product designer can now prototype the system, not just the screen.

This project shows AI-native product thinking: identifying a personal workflow problem, turning it into an information architecture, shipping a browser MVP, and designing safeguards around voice, truth and privacy.
